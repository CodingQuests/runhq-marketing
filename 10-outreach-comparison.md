# RunHQ — Outreach & GTM Comparison (us vs. Tembo, Superset, Linear)

> Created 2026-06-02. How our outreach motion compares to three near-peers, and what to copy. Sources in `02-competitor-research.md`. Re-verify before quoting publicly.

---

## TL;DR — the one pattern that matters

**All three peers grow on _inbound / pull_; our current plan is almost entirely _outbound / push_.**

- **Tembo** → SEO comparison-content at scale + founder thought-leadership + free tier + enterprise demos + OSS program.
- **Superset** → open-source (~11.5k stars) + repeated Show HN / Product Hunt launches + testimonial-driven X + Discord.
- **Linear** → product/brand-led PLG, ~**$35K total paid spend** to ~$1.25B, waitlist/build-in-public, an opinionated manifesto, a polished changelog.
- **RunHQ (planned)** → founder-to-founder cold email/DMs (100–150/wk) + value-first Reddit/LinkedIn/X posts + paid Reddit/Meta ads, quality-over-volume.

**Implication.** For a **2-week, 5-paying-customer sprint, outbound is the correct primary engine** — it's the only one that produces customers on a 14-day clock (content/OSS/brand compound over months). But every neighbor that *won* did it with a **pull engine that compounds**. So: **run our outbound sprint now, and in parallel plant 2–3 cheap inbound seeds** that keep paying off after the sprint. Don't copy Linear's "near-zero outbound" — we don't have their runway or brand yet; copy their *assets* (manifesto, craft, build-in-public), not their *patience*.

## Per-competitor teardown — and what to steal

### Tembo — *the SEO + thought-leadership machine*
- **What they do:** a blog full of high-intent **"Best X for 2026" comparison listicles** (Copilot alternatives, best AI coding agents, Devin alternatives…) that rank for category/competitor queries and conveniently list Tembo. Plus founder **Ry Walker's** prolific essay/"research" site as an education→product funnel. PLG free tier + "Book a demo" for enterprise + an OSS program for goodwill.
- **Why it works:** captures buyers at the moment of comparison; founder essays build category authority; free tier removes adoption friction.
- **Steal for RunHQ:**
  - ✅ **Publish our own comparison content** — *"RunHQ vs Tembo vs Superset vs Linear for the coding-agent loop,"* *"Best ways to ship what your AI agent writes."* Get on (or create) the SERP they own. Honest, specific, founder-written.
  - ✅ **Found­er-voice essays** on the thesis ("the bottleneck moved past writing code"; "products that self-evolve") — repurposable into the cold-outreach and organic angles we already test.
  - ⚠️ Watch them as the SEO incumbent; don't try to out-volume them — out-*specific* them (feedback→ship, founders).

### Superset — *open-source + launch-driven*
- **What they do:** source-available repo as top-of-funnel (~11.5k stars), **repeated Show HN / Launch HN** (top-4 twice) + **#1 Product Hunt**, **testimonial tweets** reshared on @superset_sh, a **1k+ Discord**, and engineering deep-dive blog posts (not SEO filler).
- **Why it works:** developers trust OSS + peer testimonials far more than ads; each launch is a free traffic spike + credibility stamp; community = compounding feedback + word-of-mouth.
- **Steal for RunHQ:**
  - ✅ **Plan a real launch moment** — a **Show HN / Product Hunt** for RunHQ (or a sharp free tool, e.g. "paste feedback → get an agent-ready task spec"). One good launch can out-deliver a week of cold DMs.
  - ✅ **Harvest + reshare testimonials** from early demos/users — social proof is the highest-converting asset for this audience.
  - ✅ Consider an **open-source or free wedge** (a CLI / the feedback→task step) as a no-friction top-of-funnel. *(Product shape confirmed 2026-06-03: hosted SaaS with a self-serve "Start free" tier — so a free entry already exists; an OSS/CLI wedge is an optional add, not a pivot.)*

### Linear — *brand / craft / build-in-public*
- **What they do:** ~**$35K total paid** to a ~$1.25B valuation. Growth from **product craft as distribution** (screenshot-worthy UI), **founder-led social**, **The Linear Method** (an opinionated manifesto that *filters for the right customers*), a **waitlist + ~1-yr closed beta + Slack community**, and a **polished, ~weekly changelog** as a content engine. Sales-assist added only later for enterprise.
- **Why it works:** an opinionated worldview attracts aligned customers and repels the rest; craft makes the product its own ad; build-in-public manufactures demand and a feedback loop.
- **Steal for RunHQ:**
  - ✅ **Write our manifesto** — a short, opinionated "RunHQ Method / why the bottleneck moved" piece. It doubles as the spine of every outreach angle and a magnet for the ICP. (We already have the raw thesis in `01`.)
  - ✅ **Build in public** on the founder's X/LinkedIn — ship notes, the self-evolve loop, real demos. Cheap, compounding, on-brand for "your product self-evolving."
  - ✅ **A crisp changelog / "what shipped" surface** once there's product motion — proof that the loop works (which the positioning doc says we *must* show before the headline promises it).
  - ⚠️ Don't mimic their *patience* (near-zero outbound) — we need the sprint's outbound now. Borrow the *assets*, not the timeline.

## Honest gap analysis

**Where ours is strong:** it's the only motion that converts on a 14-day clock; it's targeted and teaches us the winning angle/ICP fast; it's cheap; conversation-first suits a high-consideration, founder buyer.

**Where ours is weak (the gaps to close):**
1. **No pull/discovery surface** — nothing brings buyers to *us*. (Tembo's SERP, Superset's OSS, Linear's brand all do.)
2. **Absent from comparison SERPs** — Tembo is actively defining "best AI coding agent tools 2026"; we're not on the page.
3. **No social-proof flywheel** — Superset's testimonial reshares convert the same audience we're cold-DMing.
4. **Manifesto/POV unpublished** — our sharp thesis lives in internal docs, not a public magnet.
5. **No launch moment scheduled** — we're forgoing the single biggest free traffic+credibility spike (Show HN / PH).

## Recommendations

**Keep (don't change for the sprint):** the outbound cold-outreach engine, quality > volume, lead-with-pain, the A–I angle test, paid Reddit/Meta to find the winning angle fast. This is right for 5-customers-in-2-weeks.

**Add now — cheap, compounding, ~hours each (does double duty with the sprint):**
1. **Publish the manifesto** (1 post): "The bottleneck moved past writing code — here's the loop." Spine for every cold-email/organic angle. *(steal: Linear, Tembo)*
2. **Start founder build-in-public** on X/LinkedIn — demo the self-evolve loop weekly. On-brand and feeds the organic batch the routine already drafts. *(steal: Linear, Superset)*
3. **Ship one comparison post** — "RunHQ vs Tembo vs Superset vs Linear for the feedback→ship loop." Targets the SERP Tembo owns; doubles as objection-handling. *(steal: Tembo)*
4. **Harvest testimonials** from the first demos and reshare them. Highest-ROI social proof for this audience. *(steal: Superset)*

**Plan for right after the sprint (compounding bets):**
5. **A real launch moment** — Show HN / Product Hunt for RunHQ or a free wedge tool (e.g. "paste feedback → agent-ready task"). *(steal: Superset)*
6. **Decide the OSS wedge** — a self-serve "Start free" tier already exists (confirmed 2026-06-03); the open question is whether to add an OSS/CLI top-of-funnel on top of it. *(steal: Tembo, Superset)*

**Net:** Push hard outbound to hit 5 customers in 14 days; in parallel spend a few hours planting pull-engine seeds (manifesto, build-in-public, one comparison post, testimonials) so week 3 onward isn't starting from zero.
