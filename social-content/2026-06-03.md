> Here's why that doesn't surprise me. Generating code is the part that got cheap. Spend more, and you get *more code* — not more shipped product. The value never lived in generation. >

>
> → Start from **real user feedback**, not an agent inventing tasks. Most agent-PR noise is work nobody asked for.
> → End with a **structured review + preview environment**, and a human who approves the merge. Reviewed, not just generated.
>
> That loop — feedback in, reviewed PR out, shipped safely — is what we're building RunHQ around. Not full autonomy. A loop you run, that turns spend into shipped value.
